# DAX — Motion Direction (Light Mode)

# Purpose

This document defines the motion language and cinematic interaction behavior of the DAX light mode experience.

Motion is not decoration.

Motion is part of the emotional architecture of the website.

Every movement should contribute to:
- immersion
- atmosphere
- elegance
- depth
- emotional impact
- premium perception

The experience should feel alive, cinematic, and physically believable.


---

# Global Motion Philosophy

Motion should feel:
- smooth
- atmospheric
- intelligent
- luxurious
- immersive
- soft
- intentional

Avoid:
- aggressive animations
- gimmicky effects
- fast motion
- excessive movement
- distracting transitions
- playful startup interactions

The motion system should feel emotionally mature and visually sophisticated.


---

# Cinematic Motion Principles

The motion language should feel inspired by:
- cinema camera movement
- luxury brand presentations
- futuristic operating systems
- immersive installations
- high-end product reveals

Motion should create:
- anticipation
- softness
- rhythm
- visual breathing
- spatial depth

The interface should never feel static.

But movement should never become noise.


---

# Motion Timing Philosophy

Timing is critical.

Use:
- slow-medium pacing
- elegant easing
- smooth acceleration
- soft deceleration
- cinematic delay

Avoid:
- instant movement
- robotic transitions
- snappy startup animations
- exaggerated bounce

Motion should feel calm and controlled.


---

# Easing Direction

Preferred easing behavior:
- cinematic
- fluid
- natural
- physically believable

Use:
- smooth ease-out
- soft inertia
- atmospheric interpolation
- subtle momentum

Avoid:
- elastic motion
- cartoon-like bounce
- aggressive overshoot
- artificial springiness

Movement should feel premium.


---

# Spatial Motion

Elements should move through space with depth awareness.

Use:
- layered parallax
- depth shifting
- floating motion
- environmental drift
- subtle spatial response

Motion should reinforce the dimensionality of the interface.


---

The user should feel like they are moving through:
- a digital environment

- an atmospheric exhibition

Use:
- smooth scroll systems
- cinematic pacing
- visual continuity
- layered scroll behavior

- cinematic light behavior

- fog drift

- blur diffusion

---

- abrupt cuts

- disconnected composition

Use:
- atmospheric continuity
